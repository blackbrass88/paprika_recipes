Hokkaido Milk Bread

Uncategorized

Prep Time: 15 mins Cook Time: 35 mins Servings: 1 8" X 4" Loaf Source: Curiousnut.com

Ingredients

  • Tangzhong
  • 3.25 oz heavy whipping cream
  • 0.65 oz bread flour
  • Dough
  • 5.25 oz warm heavy whipping cream
  • 2 tbs sugar (depends how sweet you want your bread)
  • 2¼ tsp (1 pack) active dry yeast
  • 12.1 oz bread flour
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 room temperature egg
  • 1 tbs butter, softened
  • Egg wash
  • 1 egg
  • Some milk

Directions

1. Tangzhong: In a small pan, over medium low heat, combine the cream and flour for the tangzhong. Whisk until thick like pudding. It takes only a couple of minutes (slightly longer if using milk or water). It's ready when the temperature is 149 F or line streaks stay visible on the roux. Set aside and let cool.

2. Activate Yeast: In a small bowl, add the warm heavy whipping cream, sugar and yeast. Give it a stir and let sit for 10 minutes. This is to activate the yeast.

3. Make Dough: In a large bowl, whisk the flour and salt together. Crack the egg in, add the yeast mixture and the tangzhong. Stir until it comes together.

4. Knead: Turn it over a work surface and knead for 5 minutes. Add the butter and continue to knead. It will be messy in the beginning but adding the butter actually helps with kneading a sticky dough. Continue to knead until butter is well incorporated and dough becomes elastic another 5 minutes. The end result of the dough should be soft, smooth, elastic and tacky. When dough springs back upon poking it or when it passes the windowpane test, it's ready. If using milk or water, dough may be sticky, flour the work surface (as little as you can) just enough to get the kneading going.

5. First Rise: Allow the dough to rise (covered) in a warm, draft free place for 1 - 1½ hours or until double in size.

6. Shape: Divide the dough into 3 equal parts and shape each into a ball. Using a rolling pin, roll out the dough until it becomes a long oval. Fold one side (lengthwise) into the middle and then fold in the other side as well. Use the rolling pin and flatten it. Now, roll the dough into a cylinder. Pinch the seams. Place it seam side down into a greased 8" X 4" loaf pan. Repeat with the remaining two dough.

7. Second Rise: Cover and let rise again for 1 - 1½ hours more until double in size. 30 minutes before the loaf is ready, preheat the oven to 350 F.

8. Bake and cool: When the bread is ready to bake, brush the top of the bread with egg wash and bake for 30 minutes. Remove the bread from the pan immediately and let it cool on a wire rack.
