Posole

Soup/Stew

Prep Time: 30 minutes Cook Time: 4 hours Difficulty: Medium Servings: 6 to 8 Source: Alton Brown, I'm Just Here for the Food

Ingredients

  • Stew:
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 1/2 cups onion, chopped
  • 5 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 1/2 pound pork shoulder roast, cubed
  • 1 smoked ham hock
  • 28 ounces hominy, drained
  • 2 dried arbol or ancho chiles, chopped
  • 1 quart beef stock
  • 1 teaspoon oregano, dried
  • Kosher salt
  • Black pepper, ground
  • Garnish:
  • 1 bunch cilantro, small
  • 1 1/2 cups cabbage, thinly sliced
  • 4 radishes, small, thinly sliced
  • 2 limes, quartered

Directions

Pre-heat the oven to 275Â°.

Using 1 tablespoon of oil, sear the pork on high heat and set aside.

Drop the heat to medium-low and add the remaining oil, if necessary. Add the onion, garlic, and a heavy pinch of kosher salt and cook until translucent.

Deglaze with the beef stock, using a wooden spoon to scrape up any fond stuck to the bottom. Add the hominy, oregano, chiles, ham hock, and seared pork.

Raise the heat and bring to a boil. Cover and place in the oven for at least 4 to 5 hours.
